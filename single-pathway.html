Course Information

Doctor of Medicine and Surgery (MChD)

This is a comprehensive and varied four year course for graduate students looking to enter the rewarding field of medicine. It aims to produce graduates who are committed to compassionate, ethical health care and the expansion of medical knowledge.

The curriculum is built on important frameworks that explore the social foundations of medicine, develop understanding of the indigenous health context in Australia, and provide insights and experience in health care in rural and/or remote Australian settings. During the first two years of the course, the curriculum is built around problem based learning (PBL) tutorials as well as lectures. Students undertake a research project during Years 1 and 2. From the end of the second year, students spend almost all of their contact hours in the health sector. During third year, students again venture into the rural clinical realm, this time for six weeks. At the beginning of fourth year, students undertake an elective term of four or more weeks at a worldwide location of their choice.

Indigenous Entry Pathway

Yes, the Australian National University has an Indigenous Entry Program. Please refer to: http://medicalschool.anu.edu.au/programs-courses/mchd-doctor-medicine-and-surgery/how-apply/indigenous for more information.

In addition, you will need to tick the appropriate box on the GEMSAS application form and complete an Indigenous Australian Admission Form and send it to the ANU Medical School Admissions Office.

What assistance is available to me?

Tjabal Indigenous Higher Education Centre

Tjabal Indigenous Higher Education Centre provides a meeting place for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander students studying at the Australian National University in Canberra. Here you will find Indigenous students and staff strongly committed to improving education outcomes that benefit Australian Indigenous communities. They provide assistance with the Indigenous Tutorial Assistance Scheme â€“ ITAS and advocacy.

Assistance provided by the ANU Medical School

The ANU Medical School has a Co-ordinator of Indigenous Health and Student Engagement who provides support to Indigenous applicants by arranging tutoring and payment for GAMSAT and payment of enrolment fees. The Co-ordinator of Indigenous Health and Student Engagement provides ongoing mentoring support to Indigenous students in the ANU Medical School across the four years of their studies. They can also advise on specific scholarships available to Indigenous medical students.
